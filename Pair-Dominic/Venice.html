The Sinking City
Venice, Italy
Admire the city in all its glory from the Rialto Bridge, the oldest of the four bridges that stretch over the Grand Canal, Venice’s main thoroughfare. It’s a view that will stay with you long after you’ve left, but booking free access to the nearby Fondaco de Tedeschi’s roof terrace will give you an even better vantage point across the city. It’s best to tick off both in the early morning before they’re overrun with tourists — and don’t miss the Rialto food market on the northern side of the bridge, where you’ll witness the city’s restaurateurs inspecting the stalls for the freshest catch. Hungry? Grab a coffee and a pastry in one of the cafes on the edge of the market, or a guided food tour that starts at the market and goes on to explore all that Venice has to offer hungry visitors, seeing you through until lunch.
